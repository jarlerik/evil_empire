# Early User Feedback Strategy

## Target: Olympic Weightlifting Training Group

Starting with peers in local coached Olympic weightlifting group. Casual approach — "side project, would you try it and give feedback?" TestFlight (iPhone only).

### Approach
- Don't do a group announcement. Ask 2-3 closest people first, one-on-one
- Show it on your phone during training — let curiosity build naturally
- Frame as asking for help, not selling: "I need your honest feedback"
- Start with one person. Others will follow

### Feedback to Collect

#### Before they start using it
- "How do you track your training now?" (paper, Notes, spreadsheet, another app, nothing?)
- "What's annoying about that?"

#### First session — observe, don't explain
- Hand them the app without instructions
- Note every moment they hesitate or ask "how do I...?"
- Where they get confused = your UX feedback

#### After 1-2 weeks
- "Did you keep using it? Why / why not?" — most important question
- "What's missing for how you train?" — oly lifting specifics (complexes, percentages off maxes, blocks/cycles)
- "Would you pay for this?" — essential for PMF validation

#### What NOT to ask
- Feature wishlists — leads to noise
- Ratings or scores — meaningless with 3-5 people
- Design/color opinions — doesn't matter yet

### Key PMF Signal
**Do they keep using it without you reminding them?** That's product-market fit in its purest form.
