## Executive Summary

The Lighthouse audit gave JYM. a 92/100 SEO score with only a missing meta description flagged. That score is misleading. Lighthouse validates that tags *exist*, not that they're *useful*.

A manual inspection of the site's actual markup and structure revealed that almost every piece of on-page metadata is set to the literal string "JYM." — including the title, Open Graph title/description, and Twitter Card fields. There is no structured data, no lifestyle imagery for social sharing, and the homepage has roughly eight words of crawlable content.

That said, the gap between current state and "strong local SEO" is relatively easy to close. Karjaa is a small market, competition is minimal, and JYM.'s simulator is genuinely rare in offering. With focused effort — primarily on **metadata**, **content depth**, **schema markup**, and **Google Business Profile** — JYM. can realistically dominate local search for fitness terms and capture regional demand for the simulator offerings.

## About the Area

Karjaa (Swedish: Karis) is a bilingual town of ~9,000 people in southern Finland, part of the Raasepori (Raseborg) municipality in Uusimaa. It sits on the Helsinki–Turku coastal railway line. Understanding this market is essential for setting realistic SEO strategy:

**Small but concentrated catchment.** The town itself is ~9,000 people; adding immediate neighbors (Tammisaari/Ekenäs, Pohja/Pojo, Inkoo/Ingå, Mustio/Svartå, Hanko/Hangö, Lohja) brings the practical catchment to perhaps 30,000–40,000. Absolute organic search volume is capped, but competition is proportionally low — ranking #1 for "kuntosali Karjaa" should be very achievable.

**Genuinely bilingual.** Roughly half the population's first language is Swedish. A site that treats Swedish as a translation afterthought loses half the local market. Google Business Profile, metadata, and body content all need to be equally strong in both languages.

**Train station adjacency is underused.** JYM. sits next to Karjaa railway station on a line with direct connections to Helsinki, Turku, and Hanko. This is both a genuine accessibility feature and an SEO opportunity for practitioners and niche-interest travelers (simulator users) outside the local area.

**Two distinct geographic markets inside one business.** The fitness offering is hyper-local (people don't drive 60 km to a gym). The simulator offering is regional (hockey parents and disc golfers will drive considerably further for rare access to this equipment). **These two audiences need distinct content and SEO treatment**.

**Seasonal tourist influx.** Raasepori is a summer tourism hotspot. Tammisaari old town, Raseborg Castle ruins, Mustio Manor, the archipelago, nearby Hanko, and the region's high summer cottage density bring a large influx of domestic and foreign visitors from May through August, peaking around midsummer. This is a third distinct market that the site currently does not address at all.

## Holiday / Tourist Season Opportunity

This section is time-sensitive. As of mid-April, peak tourist season is roughly six weeks away. The window to prepare content, pricing, and local listings is narrow but workable.

### Why JYM. is genuinely well-positioned for tourists

The simulators are the single most tourist-relevant offering JYM. has, and they are not currently framed that way anywhere on the site. The positioning is natural:

**Weather resilience.** Finnish summer weather is famously unreliable. "Things to do on a rainy day" is one of the largest seasonal search query categories in Finnish tourism. Indoor activities with genuine novelty — camera-tracked hockey shooting, indoor disc golf — are exactly what visitors look for when a beach day falls through.

**Genuine novelty.** Ice hockey and disc golf simulators are rare enough in Finland that they register as an attraction in their own right, not just a service. Families, groups of friends, and couples looking for something different in the area will travel for them.

**Family-friendly and social.** The simulators work equally well for a hockey-playing kid wanting serious practice, a family looking for a fun hour together, or a group of disc golf enthusiasts escaping bad weather.

**Train station walkability.** Tourists arriving by train from Helsinki, Turku, or connecting to Hanko can reach JYM. without a rental car — a real accessibility advantage for international visitors especially, and a detail worth stating explicitly in English and Swedish content.

**Proximity to Tammisaari old town and Hanko.** Both are heavily visited in summer. A stop at JYM. fits naturally into a day-trip itinerary from either. Hanko in particular has major summer tourist volume and limited bad-weather options.

### The gym and Seeste also serve distinct tourist segments

Beyond the simulators, the gym and classes appeal to a different tourist segment: visitors staying in summer cottages or rented apartments for a week or more who want to maintain a training routine. This requires two things the site does not currently offer clearly:

- Drop-in and short-term pass pricing (day pass, weekly pass, two-week vacation pass)
- Prominent English-language information about these options

Seeste wellness services also have tourist pull — massage and therapeutic treatments are classic "holiday treat" purchases.

### Tourist search queries to target

These are the queries visitors actually type, typically in English or Swedish, sometimes in Finnish. Volume is modest but intent is very high.

- "things to do Raseborg" / "activities Karjaa"

Most have low competition from locally-relevant results, so a well-built visitor page can rank quickly.

## Current State
### Critical issues

- **Title tag is literally `JYM.`** — four characters, no keywords, no value proposition. This is the single biggest on-page SEO problem on the site.
- **Meta description missing** — known from Lighthouse.
- **All Open Graph and Twitter Card text fields read `JYM.`** — every social share preview (WhatsApp, Facebook, LinkedIn, iMessage, Slack) shows a blank-looking card with just the brand name, killing share-driven traffic.
- **No JSON-LD structured data anywhere.** No `Organization`, no `WebSite`, no `LocalBusiness`, no `Product`, no `Event`. This is the biggest technical opportunity and the single highest-leverage fix for a local business.
- **Homepage has ~8 words of crawlable content** — "Tervetuloa!", the tagline, and a "Liity nyt" button. Google cannot rank a page it can barely understand.

### Important but secondary

- `og:image` is the brand logo rather than a lifestyle/product image; logos perform poorly in social feeds.
- The primary `og:image` URL uses HTTP (the `og:image:secure_url` fallback saves it, but the main URL should be HTTPS).
- `theme-color` meta tag is present but empty.
- `og:locale` missing.
- Favicon references not visible in the head snippet inspected.
- The Swedish (`/sv`) and English (`/en`) versions almost certainly inherit the same poor metadata — half the local market loses equally.

### What's actually fine

- Canonical URL correctly set.
- Viewport and charset correct.
- `hreflang` tags present for `fi`, `sv`, `en`, and `x-default`.
- Font preloading configured.
- HTTPS sitewide except the one `og:image` URL.
- Shopify's baseline performance infrastructure is solid.

### Conclusion on current state

The site is technically healthy but semantically empty. Search engines and social platforms see a valid HTML document with almost no information about what JYM. is, what it offers, or why anyone should visit. The work to raise ranking is mostly content and metadata, not technical re-architecture.

## Strategic Framework

Three principles shape every recommendation that follows.

### 1. Two markets, two SEO strategies

Local fitness (kuntosali, ryhmätunnit) and the simulators pull from different geographic areas and serve different intents. Pages that target one should not dilute by trying to also target the other. The homepage serves both as a hub; service pages should each pick a primary audience.

### 2. Bilingual-first, not bilingual-as-afterthought

Swedish-language content deserves equal investment: separately-written metadata, separately-written body copy, a separately-set-up Google Business Profile. Machine-translating the Finnish version will produce text that doesn't match how Swedish speakers search (Swedish speakers search "gym Karis", not "kuntosali Karis"; they search "ishockeysimulator", not "jääkiekkosimulaattori").

### 3. Local search beats classical organic rank for a business this size

For a gym in a town of 9,000, Google Maps and the local 3-pack generate more real bookings than classic organic rankings. Google Business Profile, review volume and freshness, NAP (name/address/phone) consistency across the web, and `LocalBusiness` structured data on the site all matter more than long-tail blog posts.

### Instructor profiles page

#### Reasoning for the instructor profile pages

[**E-E-A-T**](https://www.semrush.com/blog/eeat/?g_network=g&g_keyword=&g_campaign=NE_SRCH_DSA_Blog_EN&g_acctid=503-093-2724&g_keywordid=dsa-2263819780519&g_adtype=search&g_adid=767109757612&g_campaignid=18350115241&g_adgroupid=153751697462&kw=&cmp=NE_SRCH_DSA_Blog_EN&label=dsa_pagefeed&Network=g&Device=c&utm_content=767109757612&kwid=dsa-2263819780519&cmpid=18350115241&agpid=153751697462&BU=Core&extid=281597275533&adpos=&matchtype=&gad_source=1&gad_campaignid=18350115241&gbraid=0AAAAADiv3HSeAPzfyN67NHOONAX4wqJqH&gclid=CjwKCAjw14zPBhAuEiwAP3-Eb_hZY_0hFQ9--mY_pHFwbWnOWQHIf1ET5E-Az46UgPp4yQP7e2EU3BoCNAwQAvD_BwE)

E-E-A-T is an acronym Google uses in its search quality guidelines. It stands for:

- **Experience** — has the author actually done the thing they're writing about?
- **Expertise** — do they have real knowledge or qualifications in the field?
- **Authoritativeness** — are they a recognized voice or reference in the topic?
- **Trustworthiness** — is the content honest, transparent, and verifiable?

**Why it matters especially for JYM.**

Google has a category of topics it calls YMYL — "Your Money or Your Life." These are topics where inaccurate or misleading information could cause real harm: medical, financial, legal, safety, nutrition, and fitness. For YMYL content, Google applies E-E-A-T standards much more strictly.

Fitness and wellness content sits squarely in YMYL. A page advising people on kickboxing technique, pilates form, or training with a hockey simulator is health-adjacent. Google wants to rank content from identifiable real people with demonstrable experience and credentials, not anonymous content written by nobody in particular.

Right now, jym.fi offers no evidence of who's behind the training. The classes are led by six named instructors, but the site doesn't introduce them. To Google, the content might as well be written by a bot.

**How instructor profiles fix this**

A `/valmentajat` page with one entry per instructor — photo, real name, short bio covering their background, years of experience, specializations, certifications, and which classes they teach — addresses all four letters:

- **Experience**: "Johanna has been teaching functional training for X years" demonstrates hands-on experience
- **Expertise**: listing certifications (personal trainer certifications, yoga teacher training, physiotherapy qualifications if applicable) demonstrates formal expertise
- **Authoritativeness**: a real named person with a photo, linkable social profiles, and possibly features in local media builds authority
- **Trustworthiness**: being transparent about who teaches what, under their real name, signals the business is operating openly and accountably

Adding `Person` schema to each profile is how you signal this to Google in machine-readable form — the schema formally declares "this is a person named Johanna, who works at JYM., whose job title is fitness instructor, with these credentials."

**Secondary benefits beyond E-E-A-T**

Even setting Google aside, instructor profiles serve business goals:

- Capture name-based searches — members often recommend classes by instructor ("ota Johannan functional-tunti"), and those searches need to land somewhere
- Give instructors a shareable page of their own, which they'll link to from their own social profiles (free backlinks, more distribution)
- Build trust with prospective members who want to know who'll be in front of them before they commit
- Support paid ads — a face-based ad with "Learn with [instructor]" outperforms a logo-based ad

**In short**

## Paid Online Marketing Strategy

Paid and organic are complementary. Organic SEO is a long-term compounding investment; paid is a fast, controllable lever for testing, seasonal pushes, and specific campaigns. JYM. should run both, but with paid carefully sized to the business.

### Budget reality check

A business of JYM.'s size and market should plan for a modest, focused paid budget — typically €400–1,500 per month baseline, with a seasonal uplift of €500–1,000 more during May–August. Spreading a small budget thinly across many channels wastes money; concentration is the right strategy.

### Strategic framing

Before any spend, four principles must be in place:

**Conversion tracking first.** Install Meta Pixel + Conversions API, Google Tag / GA4 with event tracking, and Google Ads conversion tracking before the first euro is spent. Track: page views, pricing page views, simulator page views, booking clicks, phone clicks, form submissions, and (if Shopify products are set up) completed purchases. Without this, paid spend is guessing.

**Landing page alignment.** Every paid ad must land on a page that directly matches the ad message — not the homepage. This is why the Tier 1.5 visitor page, the simulator page split, and proper pricing content matter: they are the landing pages paid ads will use.

**LTV-based thinking, not CPC-based.** A gym member is worth €600–1,200 over a year. A recurring simulator customer can be similarly valuable. Acceptable cost-per-acquisition is higher than a pure cost-per-click or cost-per-session view suggests. Report on customer lifetime value, not just immediate return.

**Language separation.** Finnish, Swedish, and English audiences respond to different copy, different imagery, and different offers. Never mix them in the same ad set. This is non-negotiable in a bilingual town targeting foreign tourists.

### Channel strategy — where to spend, and why

**Google Ads — Search (highest priority)**

The right channel for high-intent, ready-to-buy queries. Small tight campaigns on exact-match keywords. Low competition in Karjaa means cost-per-click is cheap.

Priority keyword groups:

- Local fitness: *kuntosali Karjaa*, *gym Karis*, *personal trainer Karjaa*, *kuntosali Raasepori*
- Classes: *ryhmätreenit Karjaa*, *yoga Karjaa*, *pilates Karis*, *kickboxing Karjaa*
- Simulators (regional): *jääkiekkosimulaattori*, *hockey simulator Finland*, *frisbeegolf simulaattori*, *disc golf simulator Finland*
- Seeste: *hieronta Karjaa*, *terapia Karjaa*, *vuokrattava vastaanotto Uusimaa*
- Tourist (summer): *things to do Raseborg*, *rainy day Hanko*, *activities Karjaa*

Budget split: heaviest weight on simulator + class keywords year-round, with tourist keywords added from May to August.

**Google Business Profile + Local Service Ads**

Free-first. Fully optimize GBP before paying. For physical location businesses, GBP posts, photos, reviews, and Q&A function as a free paid-like channel — they appear directly in local search results.

**Meta Ads — Facebook + Instagram (second priority)**

The right channel for awareness, discovery, and retargeting. Video creative of the simulators will significantly outperform static ads. Geotargeting plus interest targeting.

Campaign types:

- **Awareness video** to 30–50 km radius of Karjaa — simulator footage, class highlights. Goal: brand recognition.
- **Traffic campaign** to pricing page / booking flows, targeting local fitness interests.
- **Retargeting** to anyone who visited the site but didn't book — offer a first-class-free or day-pass discount.
- **Lookalike audiences** based on email list of existing members (once the list is a few hundred people).
- **Seasonal tourist campaigns** May–August, expanded geo (Helsinki metro, Sweden, Germany, Netherlands), English/Swedish creative, "rainy day activity" angle.

Meta's advantage is demographic precision: reaching specifically "parents of youth hockey players 30–50 km away" or "disc golf enthusiasts in southern Finland" is realistic.

**TikTok / Instagram Reels (high ROI for creative-led content)**

Simulator content is ideal short-form video material — novelty, slow-motion, satisfying physical feedback, clear visual hook. A single well-performing organic Reel can outperform weeks of paid spend. Paid Reels/TikTok ads amplify proven organic content; don't pay to boost content that hasn't already shown organic traction.

The Finnish disc golf community is unusually active on social video. That's free distribution if the content is good.

**YouTube Ads (lower priority, but consider for simulators)**

Pre-roll and in-stream ads on Finnish hockey and disc golf content are cheap and audience-aligned. Good for simulator-specific campaigns with 15–30s video creative. Less useful for gym/class offerings.

### Campaign structure by audience

Treat each of these as a distinct campaign set with its own budget, creative, and landing page:

**Audience 1: Local fitness seekers** (30 km radius)
- Channels: Google Search + Meta
- Offer: first class free / 7-day trial pass
- Landing: pricing page or dedicated "free trial" page
- Copy: Finnish primary, Swedish secondary

**Audience 2: Regional simulator users** (whole Uusimaa, Helsinki included)
- Channels: Google Search + Meta + YouTube
- Offer: book a simulator hour, bring a friend for free
- Landing: simulator page (eventually split into hockey / disc golf landing pages)
- Copy: Finnish primary

**Audience 3: Seasonal tourists** (May–August)
- Channels: Meta (strongest) + Google Search + TikTok Reels
- Offer: drop-in simulator hour, day pass
- Landing: `/kavijalle`, `/sv/besokare`, `/en/visit`
- Copy: English + Swedish primary; Finnish for domestic tourists
- Geo: expanded to Helsinki metro, Sweden, Germany, Netherlands

**Audience 4: Seeste wellness customers** (30 km radius)
- Channels: Google Search + Meta
- Offer: introductory treatment / first session discount
- Landing: Seeste page
- Copy: Finnish + Swedish

**Audience 5: Seeste practitioners (B2B rental)** (all Uusimaa)
- Channels: Meta (wellness professional interests) + Google Search
- Offer: flexible rental, no long commitment, tour the space
- Landing: `/seeste/vuokraa-tila` (when built)
- Copy: Finnish + Swedish

### Creative guidance

**Simulators** — slow-motion POV footage, camera-tracking data overlays, satisfying physical feedback, "this is genuinely rare in Finland" hook. A kid making an impossible shot + parent reaction is gold.

**Classes** — community feel, instructor-led, real members (with consent), energy. Avoid stock gym imagery — authenticity converts better.

**Seeste** — calm b-roll of the space, tasteful treatment footage, practitioner intro videos. The train station + accessibility angle is worth a dedicated piece of creative.

**Tourist** — "wondering what to do on a rainy day in Raseborg?" format. Family-friendly, broad demographic, clear location signal.

**Summer photo/video shoot** — commission one half-day shoot covering all of the above. Reuse across paid, organic, GBP, and PR for 6–12 months.

### Retargeting pixel setup (mandatory, do first)

- Meta Pixel with all standard events (PageView, ViewContent, Lead, Purchase) + Shopify's native integration
- Meta Conversions API for post-iOS 14 attribution
- Google Tag via Google Tag Manager with GA4 event tracking
- Google Ads conversion tracking (imported from GA4 or direct)
- Call tracking numbers on landing pages, so phone bookings are attributed to source

Set up custom audiences: all site visitors (30/60/180 day), pricing page visitors, simulator page visitors, abandoned checkout. Retargeting these audiences with a light budget is usually the most cost-effective paid activity a small business can do.

### Suggested starting monthly budget allocation

At a €500/month baseline:

- 40% Google Search (high-intent, local + simulator)
- 30% Meta (mixed awareness + retargeting)
- 20% TikTok / Reels boosts (amplifying proven organic)
- 10% experimentation / seasonal flex

At a 2x month baseline (or during tourist season):

- 35% Google Search (including English tourist queries)
- 35% Meta (including tourist-geo expansion)
- 20% TikTok / YouTube
- 10% experimentation

Scale up only after the baseline is producing measurable return.

### What to avoid

- "Boost post" button on Facebook — optimizes for the wrong metric. Always use Ads Manager.
- Broad Performance Max campaigns without tight exclusions — can burn budget on irrelevant placements.
- Generic brand awareness campaigns without a conversion goal.
- Running ads to the current homepage (8 words of content, no clear action).
- Agencies without a track record in fitness, wellness, or local businesses.
- Long-term commitments or retainers before proof of return.

### Measurement cadence

Weekly: scan for obvious waste (campaigns with zero conversions at high spend), kill underperformers.

Monthly: report by channel — spend, conversions, cost per conversion, revenue attributed, top creative, landing page performance.

Quarterly: recompute LTV based on actual retention data, adjust acceptable CAC targets.

## Final Note

JYM has an unusually strong set of fundamentals for a small-town business — a distinctive offering (the simulators), a bilingual market opportunity, a genuine accessibility advantage (the train station), minimal local competition, and a seasonal tourist market that the competition is not targeting. The work to turn that into real search visibility is straightforward; most of it is writing, not engineering. The one deadline-driven workstream is tourist-season preparation: the six-week window before peak season is the single highest-leverage period on the calendar, and the prep cost is low compared to the year-round traffic and revenue it unlocks.
